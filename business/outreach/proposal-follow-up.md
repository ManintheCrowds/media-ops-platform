# Proposal Follow-Up Sequence

Structured follow-up sequence for proposals to maximize response rates and close deals.

## Follow-Up Timeline

### Day 1: Send Proposal
- Send proposal via email
- Include clear next steps
- Set expectations for response time

### Day 3-4: First Follow-Up
- Check if they received proposal
- Answer any initial questions
- Offer to discuss

### Day 7: Second Follow-Up
- Share additional value (case study, resource)
- Reiterate key benefits
- Offer flexible timing

### Day 14: Third Follow-Up
- Final check-in
- Offer to adjust proposal if needed
- Set deadline if appropriate

### Day 30: Final Follow-Up
- Close the loop
- Offer to reconnect in future
- Ask for feedback

## Phone Follow-Up Scripts

### Script 1: Initial Follow-Up Call

**Opening:**
"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from Xanadu Media. I sent you a proposal for [Project Name] earlier this week. Do you have a few minutes to chat?"

**If Yes:**
"Great! I wanted to make sure you received it and see if you have any questions."

**If No:**
"No problem. When would be a better time? I'm available [options]."

**Questions to Ask:**
- "Have you had a chance to review the proposal?"
- "Do you have any questions about the scope or approach?"
- "What's your timeline for making a decision?"
- "Is there anything I can clarify or adjust?"

**Closing:**
"Thanks for your time. I'll follow up via email with [next steps]. Looking forward to working together!"

---

### Script 2: Addressing Concerns

**Opening:**
"Hi [First Name], I wanted to address [specific concern] you mentioned about the proposal."

**Acknowledge:**
"I understand [concern]. That's a valid point, and here's how we can address it: [solution]."

**Offer Options:**
"We have a few options:
1. [Option 1]
2. [Option 2]
3. [Option 3]

Which approach works best for you?"

**Closing:**
"Great! I'll update the proposal with [agreed solution] and send it over by [timeframe]. Does that work?"

## Handling Common Responses

### Response: "We're Still Reviewing"

**Email Response:**

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for the update! I completely understand you need time to review.

If it would be helpful, I'm happy to:
- Schedule a call to answer any questions
- Provide additional information or case studies
- Adjust the proposal based on your feedback

Just let me know what would be most helpful. I'll follow up in [timeframe] if I don't hear from you.

Best,  
[Your Name]

---

### Response: "It's Too Expensive"

**Email Response:**

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for the feedback. I understand budget is a consideration.

I'd be happy to discuss options:
- Phased approach (start with Phase 1)
- Adjusted scope to fit budget
- Payment terms that work for you
- Value-focused alternatives

Would you be open to a quick call to explore options?

Best,  
[Your Name]

---

### Response: "The Timeline Doesn't Work"

**Email Response:**

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for letting me know. I'd be happy to adjust the timeline to better fit your needs.

Could you share:
- Your ideal start date?
- Your target completion date?
- Any key milestones or deadlines?

Based on that, I can revise the proposal with a timeline that works for you.

Best,  
[Your Name]

---

### Response: "We're Going with Another Vendor"

**Email Response:**

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for letting me know. I appreciate you taking the time to consider Xanadu Media.

If you're open to it, I'd love to hear any feedback about the proposal or process. It helps me improve for future opportunities.

If things don't work out with the other vendor, or if you have future projects, I'm here to help.

Best of luck with [Project Name]!

Best,  
[Your Name]

## Best Practices

### Timing

- **First Follow-Up:** 3-4 days after sending
- **Second Follow-Up:** 1 week after sending
- **Third Follow-Up:** 2 weeks after sending
- **Final Follow-Up:** 1 month after sending

### Approach

- **Be Persistent, Not Pushy:** Follow up regularly but respectfully
- **Add Value:** Each follow-up should offer something valuable
- **Be Flexible:** Offer to adjust proposal based on feedback
- **Respect Their Time:** Keep messages brief and to the point
- **Know When to Stop:** After final follow-up, move on

### Communication Channels

- **Email:** Primary channel for follow-ups
- **Phone:** For important discussions or if email isn't working
- **LinkedIn:** Light touch, less formal
- **In-Person:** If local and appropriate

### Tracking

- **CRM System:** Track all follow-ups and responses
- **Calendar Reminders:** Set reminders for follow-up dates
- **Notes:** Document conversations and feedback
- **Status Updates:** Update proposal status regularly

## Metrics to Track

- **Response Rate:** % of proposals that get responses
- **Conversion Rate:** % of proposals that convert to projects
- **Average Response Time:** How long until first response
- **Follow-Up Effectiveness:** Which follow-ups get responses
- **Common Objections:** Track and address common concerns
