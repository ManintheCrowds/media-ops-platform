# LLC Setup Guide

This guide provides step-by-step instructions for setting up a Limited Liability Company (LLC) in the United States. **Note: This is informational only. Consult with a qualified attorney and accountant for legal and tax advice specific to your situation.**

## Overview

An LLC (Limited Liability Company) provides:
- **Limited Liability:** Personal assets protected from business debts
- **Tax Flexibility:** Can be taxed as sole proprietorship, partnership, or corporation
- **Simplicity:** Less formal than a corporation
- **Credibility:** Professional business structure

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Choose Your State

**Considerations:**
- **Home State:** Simplest option, may have lower fees
- **Delaware:** Popular for businesses, but may require registered agent in DE
- **Wyoming/Nevada:** Low fees, privacy-friendly, but may need registered agent

**Recommendation:** Start with your home state for simplicity.

### Step 2: Choose a Business Name

**Requirements:**
- Must be unique in your state
- Must include "LLC" or "Limited Liability Company"
- Cannot conflict with existing businesses
- Check availability on state business database

**Tips:**
- Choose SEO-friendly name if applicable
- Check domain name availability
- Consider trademark implications
- Keep it simple and memorable

**Name Reservation (Optional):**
- Some states allow name reservation (usually 60-120 days)
- Fee: $10-50 typically
- Useful if you're not ready to file immediately

### Step 3: Designate a Registered Agent

**Requirements:**
- Must have physical address in state of formation
- Must be available during business hours
- Can be yourself, another person, or professional service

**Options:**
- **Yourself:** Free, but requires physical address and availability
- **Professional Service:** $50-300/year, handles all official mail

**Recommended Services:**
- Northwest Registered Agent
- Incfile
- ZenBusiness

### Step 4: File Articles of Organization

**What to File:**
- Articles of Organization (or Certificate of Formation)
- Filed with your state's Secretary of State

**Information Required:**
- Business name
- Registered agent name and address
- Business purpose (can be general)
- Management structure (member-managed or manager-managed)
- Duration (usually perpetual)
- Organizer information

**Filing Methods:**
- Online (fastest, usually same-day)
- Mail (slower, 1-2 weeks)
- In-person (if available)

**Fees:**
- Vary by state: $50-500 typically
- Some states have annual fees

### Step 5: Create an Operating Agreement

**What It Is:**
- Internal document governing LLC operations
- Not always required by state, but highly recommended

**Contents:**
- Member information and ownership percentages
- Management structure
- Profit/loss distribution
- Voting rights
- Transfer restrictions
- Dissolution procedures

**Templates:**
- Available online (LegalZoom, Rocket Lawyer)
- Should be reviewed by attorney
- Customize for your specific needs

### Step 6: Obtain EIN (Employer Identification Number)

**What It Is:**
- Federal tax ID number (like SSN for business)
- Required for opening business bank account
- Required if you have employees

**How to Get:**
- **Online:** IRS website (free, immediate)
- **Fax:** Form SS-4 (4 business days)
- **Mail:** Form SS-4 (4-5 weeks)

**When Needed:**
- Opening business bank account
- Hiring employees
- Filing business tax returns
- Some states require for LLC registration

### Step 7: Open Business Bank Account

**Requirements:**
- EIN (from Step 6)
- Articles of Organization
- Operating Agreement
- Business license (if required)

**Benefits:**
- Separate business and personal finances
- Professional appearance
- Easier accounting and taxes
- Liability protection

**Recommended Banks:**
- Local credit union (often lower fees)
- Online banks (Chase, Bank of America)
- Business-specific banks (Mercury, Novo)

### Step 8: Obtain Business Licenses and Permits

**Types:**
- **General Business License:** Required in some cities/counties
- **Professional Licenses:** If applicable to your industry
- **Sales Tax Permit:** If selling products/services

**How to Find:**
- Check city/county websites
- State business portal
- SBA.gov business license search

### Step 9: Set Up Accounting System

**Options:**
- **QuickBooks:** Most popular, $25-150/month
- **Xero:** Good alternative, $13-70/month
- **Wave:** Free (good for small businesses)
- **FreshBooks:** Good for service businesses

**Recommendation:** Start with Wave (free) or QuickBooks Self-Employed ($25/month)

### Step 10: Get Business Insurance

**Types:**
- **General Liability:** Protects against claims
- **Professional Liability (E&O):** For service businesses
- **Business Property:** If you have equipment/office

**Recommended Providers:**
- Hiscox
- Next Insurance
- State Farm

## State-Specific Information

### Popular States for LLC Formation

**Delaware:**
- Pros: Business-friendly, well-established law
- Cons: Annual franchise tax, may need registered agent
- Fee: $90 filing + $300 annual tax

**Wyoming:**
- Pros: Low fees, privacy-friendly, no state income tax
- Cons: May need registered agent
- Fee: $60 filing + $60 annual report

**Nevada:**
- Pros: No state income tax, privacy-friendly
- Cons: Higher fees, may need registered agent
- Fee: $425 filing + $350 annual list

**Your Home State:**
- Pros: Simplest, no registered agent needed (usually)
- Cons: May have higher taxes
- Fee: Varies by state

## Timeline and Costs

### Typical Timeline
- **Name Check:** Same day
- **Filing:** 1-7 business days (online) or 2-4 weeks (mail)
- **EIN:** Immediate (online) or 4-5 weeks (mail)
- **Bank Account:** 1-2 weeks after EIN
- **Total:** 2-4 weeks from start to finish

### Typical Costs
- **State Filing Fee:** $50-500
- **Registered Agent:** $0-300/year (if using service)
- **Operating Agreement:** $0-200 (template or attorney)
- **EIN:** Free
- **Business License:** $0-400 (varies by location)
- **Bank Account:** Usually free
- **Total First Year:** $100-1,500 (depending on state and services)

## Post-Formation Requirements

### Annual Requirements

**Annual Report/Statement:**
- Most states require annual filing
- Fee: $0-400 typically
- Due date: Varies by state

**Tax Requirements:**
- Federal tax return (Form 1065 if multi-member, Schedule C if single-member)
- State tax return (if applicable)
- Estimated quarterly taxes (if applicable)

**Record Keeping:**
- Maintain operating agreement
- Keep meeting minutes (if applicable)
- Maintain financial records
- Keep business and personal finances separate

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Mixing Personal and Business Finances:** Always use separate bank account
2. **Not Having Operating Agreement:** Essential for multi-member LLCs
3. **Not Getting EIN:** Required for business bank account
4. **Forgetting Annual Filings:** Can result in penalties or dissolution
5. **Not Consulting Professionals:** Attorney and accountant are worth the cost
