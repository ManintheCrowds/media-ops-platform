## Best Practices

### Connection Requests

- **Personalize:** Always personalize, never use default message
- **Be Brief:** Keep it short and friendly
- **Add Value:** Mention why you want to connect
- **No Sales Pitch:** Don't sell in connection request

### Messages

- **Wait:** Wait a few days after connecting before sending message
- **Personalize:** Reference their profile, company, or recent activity
- **Add Value:** Offer something valuable (insight, resource, case study)
- **Be Brief:** Keep messages concise
- **Clear CTA:** Have a clear call-to-action

### Timing

- **Best Days:** Tuesday-Thursday
- **Best Times:** 9-10 AM, 2-3 PM
- **Avoid:** Monday mornings, Friday afternoons
- **Response Time:** Respond within 24 hours

### Profile Optimization

- **Professional Photo:** Clear, professional headshot
- **Compelling Headline:** Include value proposition
- **Complete Profile:** Fill out all sections
- **Keywords:** Use relevant keywords for searchability
- **Recommendations:** Get recommendations from clients

### Content Strategy

- **Share Value:** Post helpful content regularly
- **Engage:** Comment on others' posts
- **Showcase Work:** Share case studies and results
- **Industry Insights:** Share relevant industry news
- **Personal Brand:** Show your expertise and personality

- **LinkedIn Premium:** More InMail credits, advanced search

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Generic Messages:** Always personalize
2. **Too Salesy:** Focus on value, not sales
3. **Too Long:** Keep messages brief
4. **No Follow-Up:** Follow up appropriately
5. **Ignoring Profile:** Research before reaching out
6. **Spammy Behavior:** Don't send too many messages
7. **No Value:** Always offer something valuable
