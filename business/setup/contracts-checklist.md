# Contracts and Legal Documents Checklist

This checklist outlines the essential contracts and legal documents needed for a freelance development business.

## Essential Contracts

### 1. Service Agreement / Master Service Agreement

**Purpose:** Main contract governing client relationships

**Key Sections:**
- Services description
- Payment terms
- Timeline and milestones
- Intellectual property
- Confidentiality
- Warranties and disclaimers
- Limitation of liability
- Termination clauses

**When Needed:** Before starting any client work

**Template Location:** `business/templates/contract-template.md`

---

### 2. Statement of Work (SOW)

**Purpose:** Detailed project scope document

**Key Sections:**
- Project objectives
- Detailed scope
- Deliverables
- Timeline and milestones
- Acceptance criteria
- Pricing

**When Needed:** For each project (attached to Service Agreement)

**Template Location:** `business/templates/statement-of-work-template.md`

---

### 3. Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

**Purpose:** Protect confidential information

**Key Sections:**
- Definition of confidential information
- Obligations of receiving party
- Exceptions
- Duration
- Remedies

**When Needed:** Before sharing sensitive information

**Types:**
- **Mutual NDA:** Both parties share confidential info
- **One-Way NDA:** Only one party shares info

**Template:** Available online (LegalZoom, Rocket Lawyer) or consult attorney

---

### 4. Independent Contractor Agreement

**Purpose:** If you hire subcontractors

**Key Sections:**
- Services description
- Payment terms
- Independent contractor status
- Intellectual property
- Confidentiality
- Non-compete (if applicable)

**When Needed:** When hiring freelancers or contractors

---

## Important Legal Documents

### 5. Terms of Service / Terms and Conditions

**Purpose:** Govern use of your website/services

**Key Sections:**
- Service description
- User obligations
- Payment terms
- Intellectual property
- Limitation of liability
- Dispute resolution

**When Needed:** If you have a website or SaaS product

**Where to Place:** Website footer, linked from main pages

---

### 6. Privacy Policy

**Purpose:** Required by law (GDPR, CCPA) if collecting user data

**Key Sections:**
- Data collection practices
- How data is used
- Data sharing
- User rights
- Contact information

**When Needed:** If collecting any user data (even email addresses)

**Where to Place:** Website footer, linked from main pages

**Resources:**
- Privacy Policy Generator (free online tools)
- Consult attorney for compliance

---

### 7. Website Disclaimer

**Purpose:** Limit liability for website content

**Key Sections:**
- No warranties
- Limitation of liability
- External links disclaimer
- Professional advice disclaimer

**When Needed:** For any business website

**Where to Place:** Website footer

---

## Business Formation Documents

### 8. Articles of Organization (LLC)

**Purpose:** Official LLC formation document

**Filed With:** State Secretary of State

**When Needed:** When forming LLC

**See:** `business/setup/llc-setup-guide.md`

---

### 9. Operating Agreement (LLC)

**Purpose:** Internal governance document for LLC

**Key Sections:**
- Member information
- Management structure
- Profit/loss distribution
- Voting rights

**When Needed:** When forming LLC (highly recommended)

**Template:** Available online or consult attorney

---

## Financial Documents

### 10. Invoice Template

**Purpose:** Professional invoicing

**Key Elements:**
- Invoice number
- Date
- Client information
- Services description
- Amount due
- Payment terms
- Payment methods

**When Needed:** For every client project

**Tools:** QuickBooks, FreshBooks, Wave, or custom template

---

### 11. W-9 Form (for US contractors)

**Purpose:** Tax information for clients

**When Needed:** If working with US companies (they may request)

**Where to Get:** IRS.gov (Form W-9)

---

## Insurance Documents

### 12. Certificate of Insurance

**Purpose:** Proof of insurance coverage

**When Needed:** Some clients require proof of insurance

**Types:**
- General Liability
- Professional Liability (E&O)
- Cyber Liability

---

## Document Management

### Organization System

**Recommended Structure:**
```
business/
├── contracts/
│   ├── signed/
│   │   ├── client-name-service-agreement-2024-01-15.pdf
│   │   └── client-name-sow-2024-01-15.pdf
│   └── templates/
├── legal/
│   ├── llc-formation/
│   ├── insurance/
│   └── tax/
└── invoices/
    ├── 2024/
    └── templates/
```

### Best Practices

1. **Digital Storage:** Store all documents digitally (cloud backup)
2. **Naming Convention:** Use consistent naming (client-name-document-type-date)
3. **Version Control:** Keep versions of contracts before changes
4. **Backup:** Regular backups of all documents
5. **Access Control:** Secure storage for sensitive documents
6. **Retention:** Keep documents for required retention period (consult attorney)

## Legal Review Checklist

Before using any contract template:

- [ ] Reviewed by attorney (recommended for first use)
- [ ] Customized for your specific business
- [ ] State-specific requirements checked
- [ ] Industry-specific requirements checked
- [ ] Terms are fair and enforceable
- [ ] All blanks filled in
- [ ] Both parties have signed copies

---

## When to Consult an Attorney

**Definitely Consult:**
- First time creating contracts
- Complex projects or high-value contracts
- International clients
- Intellectual property concerns
- Employment/contractor relationships
- Dispute resolution

**May Not Need:**
- Simple, low-value projects (but still recommended)
- Using well-reviewed templates
- Standard service agreements

**Cost:** Attorney review typically $200-500/hour, but worth it for protection

## Important Reminders

1. **Never Sign Without Reading:** Always read contracts thoroughly
2. **Get Everything in Writing:** Verbal agreements are hard to enforce
3. **Keep Copies:** Both parties should have signed copies
4. **Review Regularly:** Update contracts as business evolves
5. **Consult Professionals:** Attorney and accountant are worth the investment
