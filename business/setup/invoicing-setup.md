# Invoicing Setup Guide

This guide covers setting up an invoicing system for your freelance development business.

## Overview

A professional invoicing system is essential for:
- Getting paid on time
- Tracking income
- Tax preparation
- Professional appearance
- Cash flow management

## Invoicing Software Options

**Features:**
- Professional invoices
- Time tracking
- Expense tracking
- Client portal
- Payment processing
- Reports

**Pricing:** $17-55/month

**Pros:**

- More expensive than some alternatives
- Limited customization

## Invoice Template Elements

### Required Elements

1. **Invoice Number:** Unique identifier (e.g., INV-2024-001)
2. **Date:** Invoice date
3. **Due Date:** Payment due date
4. **Your Business Information:**
   - Business name
   - Address
   - Phone
   - Email
   - Website
5. **Client Information:**
   - Client name
   - Address
   - Contact person
6. **Services Description:**
   - Service/item description
   - Quantity
   - Rate
   - Amount
7. **Totals:**
   - Subtotal
   - Taxes (if applicable)
   - Total
8. **Payment Terms:**
   - Payment methods accepted
   - Payment terms (Net 15, Net 30, etc.)
   - Late payment policy
9. **Payment Instructions:**
   - How to pay
   - Payment address/account

### Optional Elements

- Logo
- Terms and conditions
- Notes/thank you message
- Purchase order number
- Project reference

---

## Invoice Numbering System

### Recommended Format

**Format:** `INV-YYYY-NNN`

**Example:** `INV-2024-001`

**Benefits:**
- Chronological
- Easy to track
- Professional
- Prevents duplicates

### Alternative Formats

- `[Client]-YYYY-NNN` (e.g., `ACME-2024-001`)
- `PROJ-YYYY-NNN` (e.g., `PROJ-2024-001`)
- Sequential: `001`, `002`, `003`

---

## Payment Terms

### Common Terms

- **Net 15:** Payment due within 15 days
- **Net 30:** Payment due within 30 days (most common)
- **Due on Receipt:** Payment due immediately
- **50% Upfront, 50% on Completion:** Common for projects

### Recommendations

- **For New Clients:** Net 15 or upfront payment
- **For Established Clients:** Net 30
- **For Large Projects:** Milestone-based payments

---

## Payment Methods

### Options to Accept

1. **Bank Transfer/ACH:**
   - Pros: Low fees, direct deposit
   - Cons: Slower processing

2. **Credit Card:**
   - Pros: Fast, convenient for clients
   - Cons: Processing fees (2.9% + $0.30 typical)

3. **Check:**
   - Pros: No fees
   - Cons: Slow, risk of bouncing

4. **PayPal:**
   - Pros: Widely used, easy
   - Cons: Fees (2.9% + $0.30)

5. **Stripe:**
   - Pros: Professional, good for online
   - Cons: Fees (2.9% + $0.30)

### Recommendation

Accept multiple methods:
- Bank transfer for large payments
- Credit card for convenience (use Stripe or PayPal)
- Check as backup

## Invoice Workflow

### 1. Create Invoice

- Use template
- Fill in client and project details
- Add line items
- Set payment terms
- Review for accuracy

### 2. Send Invoice

- Send via email (PDF attachment)
- Include clear subject line
- Add professional message
- CC yourself for records

### 3. Track Invoice

- Mark as sent
- Set reminder for follow-up
- Track payment status

### 4. Follow Up

- Send reminder 3-5 days before due date
- Send overdue notice if past due
- Escalate if significantly overdue

### 5. Record Payment

- Mark invoice as paid
- Record payment method
- Update accounting records
- Send receipt/thank you

---

## Best Practices

### 1. Send Invoices Promptly

- Send immediately upon completion
- Don't delay invoicing
- Set up automatic invoicing if possible

### 2. Be Clear and Professional

- Use professional template
- Clear descriptions
- Accurate amounts
- Professional language

### 3. Set Clear Payment Terms

- State terms clearly
- Include late payment policy
- Specify payment methods

### 4. Follow Up Systematically

- Automated reminders
- Consistent follow-up schedule
- Professional but firm tone

### 5. Keep Records

- Save all invoices (sent and paid)
- Track payment dates
- Maintain organized filing system

## Legal Considerations

### Invoice Requirements

- Must include business name and address
- Must include invoice number and date
- Must clearly state amount due
- Must include payment terms

### Tax Considerations

- Keep all invoices for tax records
- Track taxable vs. non-taxable services
- Maintain organized records
- Consult accountant for tax advice

## Troubleshooting

### Client Not Paying

1. **Send Reminder:** Polite reminder email
2. **Follow Up:** Phone call if appropriate
3. **Late Fee:** Apply late fee if in contract
4. **Escalate:** Consider collection agency or legal action

### Payment Disputes

1. **Review Invoice:** Check for errors
2. **Communicate:** Discuss with client
3. **Document:** Keep all communications
4. **Resolve:** Work toward resolution
5. **Legal Action:** Last resort
