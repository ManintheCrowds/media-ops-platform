# Accounting Setup Guide

This guide covers setting up an accounting system for your freelance development business.

## Overview

Proper accounting is essential for:
- Tax compliance
- Financial tracking
- Business decision-making
- Cash flow management
- Professional credibility

## Accounting Software Options

### 1. QuickBooks

**Best For:** Businesses needing full-featured accounting

**Features:**
- Full double-entry accounting
- Invoicing
- Expense tracking
- Bank reconciliation
- Financial reports
- Tax preparation
- Payroll (add-on)

**Pricing:** $30-200/month

**Plans:**
- **Simple Start:** $30/month - Basic accounting
- **Essentials:** $55/month - Multiple users
- **Plus:** $85/month - Advanced features
- **Advanced:** $200/month - Enterprise features

**Pros:**
- Industry standard
- Comprehensive features
- Good integrations
- Strong reporting
- Tax preparation tools

**Cons:**
- Can be complex
- More expensive
- Steep learning curve
- May be overkill for simple businesses

## Recommended Setup

**Why:**
- Free accounting software
- Good enough for most freelancers
- Can export data if you need to upgrade

**Setup:**
1. Sign up at waveapps.com
2. Create business profile
3. Connect bank account (optional)
4. Set up chart of accounts
5. Start tracking income and expenses

## Bank Reconciliation

### Process

1. **Get Bank Statement:** Download from bank
2. **Compare Transactions:** Match accounting records to bank statement
3. **Identify Differences:** Find unmatched transactions
4. **Reconcile:** Mark transactions as reconciled
5. **Resolve Discrepancies:** Investigate and fix differences

### Best Practices

- Reconcile monthly (minimum)
- Reconcile weekly (recommended)
- Keep bank statements organized
- Investigate discrepancies immediately

---

## Financial Reports

### Essential Reports

1. **Profit & Loss (P&L) / Income Statement:**
   - Shows income and expenses
   - Shows profit/loss
   - Run monthly

2. **Balance Sheet:**
   - Shows assets, liabilities, equity
   - Shows financial position
   - Run monthly

3. **Cash Flow Statement:**
   - Shows cash inflows and outflows
   - Shows cash position
   - Run monthly

4. **Accounts Receivable Aging:**
   - Shows outstanding invoices
   - Shows payment status
   - Run weekly

### When to Review

- **Weekly:** Cash flow, accounts receivable
- **Monthly:** P&L, balance sheet, cash flow
- **Quarterly:** All reports, tax planning
- **Annually:** Full financial review

## Tax Considerations

### Record Keeping

- Keep all receipts (digital or physical)
- Keep all invoices
- Keep bank statements
- Keep tax documents
- Organize by year

### Deductible Expenses

Common deductions for freelancers:
- Home office (if applicable)
- Software subscriptions
- Equipment (computers, etc.)
- Internet/phone
- Professional services
- Marketing/advertising
- Travel (business-related)
- Meals (business-related, 50% deductible)
- Education/training
- Insurance

**Important:** Consult with accountant for tax advice

### Estimated Taxes

- Required if you expect to owe $1,000+ in taxes
- Pay quarterly (April, June, September, January)
- Based on previous year's income
- Consult accountant for calculation

## Best Practices

### 1. Separate Business and Personal

- Use separate bank account
- Use separate credit card
- Never mix personal and business expenses

### 2. Record Everything

- Record all income
- Record all expenses
- Keep receipts
- Don't skip small expenses

### 3. Reconcile Regularly

- Reconcile bank accounts monthly
- Reconcile credit cards monthly
- Investigate discrepancies immediately

### 4. Review Regularly

- Review financial reports monthly
- Check cash flow weekly
- Plan for taxes quarterly

### 5. Get Professional Help

- Consult accountant for tax advice
- Consider bookkeeper for complex situations
- Review with accountant annually
